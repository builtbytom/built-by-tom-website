<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">If you've Googled anything lately, you might have noticed something new: AI-powered results are now front and center. Google's using artificial intelligence to answer questions directly, summarize websites, and even recommend businesses before anyone clicks a single link.</p>

<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">For local business owners, that's a big shift. Here's what's actually happening, and what you should do about it.</p>

<h2 class="font-display font-bold text-2xl lg:text-3xl text-foreground mb-6 mt-12">Why This Matters for Small Businesses</h2>

<h3 class="font-bold text-xl lg:text-2xl text-primary mb-4 mt-10 pb-2 border-b border-gray-100">1. "Zero Click" Is the New Normal</h3>
<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">People are getting their answers faster than ever, and often don't click through to a website at all. If your business info isn't in the "right place," you're invisible, no matter how nice your site is.</p>

<h3 class="font-bold text-xl lg:text-2xl text-primary mb-4 mt-10 pb-2 border-b border-gray-100">2. Reviews and Real-World Data Matter More</h3>
<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">Google's AI loves real reviews, accurate info, and up-to-date profiles. If your Google Business Profile is out of date, you won't show up in these summaries.</p>

<h3 class="font-bold text-xl lg:text-2xl text-primary mb-4 mt-10 pb-2 border-b border-gray-100">3. Authority and Trust Signals Rule</h3>
<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">AI pulls from sources it trusts. Think Google Business, Yelp, your own site, and strong local mentions. If you look inconsistent or spammy, you get ignored.</p>
