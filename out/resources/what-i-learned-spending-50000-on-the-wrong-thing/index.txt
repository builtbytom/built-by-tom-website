<h2 class="font-display font-bold text-2xl lg:text-3xl text-foreground mb-6 mt-12">The Invisible Money Drain</h2>

<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">For the first few years of my business, I thought we were doing pretty well. Sales were growing, we had employees, things seemed to be moving in the right direction.</p>

<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">But underneath the surface, we were bleeding money in ways I didn't even realize.</p>

<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">Orders would come in and sit for days before anyone processed them. Inventory would arrive and never get checked in properly. We'd run out of products we thought we had plenty of. Employees would spend hours looking for things that should have taken minutes to find.</p>

<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">It wasn't until I added it all up that I realized: <strong class="font-bold text-foreground">our lack of systems was costing us more than $50,000 a year in lost sales, wasted time, and pure inefficiency.</strong></p>

<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">Here's what that looked like in real numbers - and why it matters for your business too.</p>

<h2 class="font-display font-bold text-2xl lg:text-3xl text-foreground mb-6 mt-12">The Hidden Costs of "Winging It"</h2>

<h3 class="font-bold text-xl lg:text-2xl text-primary mb-4 mt-10 pb-2 border-b border-gray-100">Missed Sales from Inventory Chaos</h3>

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">The Problem:</strong> Orders would come in, but nobody had a system for checking them against what we actually received. We'd think we had 100 units of something when we really had 20.

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">The Cost:</strong> Customers would order products we didn't actually have. By the time we figured it out, they'd already gone to a competitor. We estimated this cost us $15,000+ in lost sales annually.

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">The Fix:</strong> Simple inventory tracking. Every shipment gets checked in immediately. Every employee knows the process. No exceptions.

<h3 class="font-bold text-xl lg:text-2xl text-primary mb-4 mt-10 pb-2 border-b border-gray-100">Labor Costs Through the Roof</h3>

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">The Problem:</strong> Without clear processes, everything took three times longer than it should. Employees would spend 20 minutes looking for a product that should have been in a designated spot.

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">The Cost:</strong> We were paying 4 people to do the work of 2. That's an extra $40,000+ in unnecessary labor costs.

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">The Fix:</strong> Designated locations for everything. Clear workflows. If someone can't find something in 2 minutes, the system is broken.

<h3 class="font-bold text-xl lg:text-2xl text-primary mb-4 mt-10 pb-2 border-b border-gray-100">Shipments Sitting in Limbo</h3>

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">The Problem:</strong> Products would arrive at our warehouse, but without a clear process for prep and shipping to Amazon's fulfillment centers. Boxes would sit for days while we figured out what to do with them.

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">The Cost:</strong> Amazon customers ordering products we technically had, but hadn't sent in yet. Plus storage fees and delayed revenue while inventory sat in our warehouse instead of earning money.

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">The Fix:</strong> Immediate receiving process. Everything gets prepped and shipped to Amazon within 24 hours of arrival.

<h2 class="font-display font-bold text-2xl lg:text-3xl text-foreground mb-6 mt-12">What This Looks Like in a Small Business</h2>

<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">Maybe you're not running a warehouse, but I bet you recognize these problems:</p>

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">In a restaurant:</strong> Orders come in, but there's no system for tracking inventory. You run out of the special halfway through dinner service because nobody knew how much you actually had.

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">In a service business:</strong> Client requests come in via email, text, and phone calls. Some get handled immediately, others sit for weeks because they fell through the cracks.

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">In an e-commerce business:</strong> Products arrive but sit in your warehouse for days without being processed and sent to fulfillment centers. Customers are ordering things you have, but can't sell because they're stuck in your prep process.

<strong class="font-bold text-foreground">In any business:</strong> You're paying people to hunt for information that should be organized. Everyone's working harder, not smarter.

<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">The solution wasn't complicated. It was just systematic:</p>

<h3 class="font-bold text-xl lg:text-2xl text-primary mb-4 mt-10 pb-2 border-b border-gray-100">1. Document Everything</h3>
<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">Write down your process for receiving orders, checking inventory, fulfilling requests - everything. If it's not written down, it doesn't exist.</p>

<h3 class="font-bold text-xl lg:text-2xl text-primary mb-4 mt-10 pb-2 border-b border-gray-100">2. Make One Person Responsible</h3>
<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">Every process needs an owner. Not "whoever's available" - one specific person who's accountable.</p>

<h3 class="font-bold text-xl lg:text-2xl text-primary mb-4 mt-10 pb-2 border-b border-gray-100">3. Check Your System Daily</h3>
<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">Set aside 15 minutes every morning to make sure nothing fell through the cracks. Small problems are easy to fix. Big problems cost money.</p>

<h3 class="font-bold text-xl lg:text-2xl text-primary mb-4 mt-10 pb-2 border-b border-gray-100">4. Train Everyone the Same Way</h3>
<p class="text-lg text-text-light leading-relaxed mb-6">If three different employees do something three different ways, you don't have a system - you have chaos.</p>
