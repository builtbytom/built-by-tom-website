🐕 Built for Pet Groomers

"Fluffy Dogs, Tangled Systems"...and You're Doing Everything but What You Actually Love

Running a pet grooming business is way more than bubbles and bows. It's chasing no-shows, dealing with "can you fit my doodle in right now?" calls, and trying to text updates while wrestling a squirming poodle. Some days you feel like a groomer, a therapist, and an air traffic controller all at once. You got into this to make pets look amazing—not to spend half your day managing chaos.

😩

The Real Headaches Pet Groomers Deal With

💧 You Can't Answer the Phone with Wet Hands

You're shampooing a golden retriever. Phone rings. You can't answer. By the time you dry off, they've booked with the place that picked up. Every missed call is money walking out the door. But you can't drop everything every time someone calls, especially when you're holding scissors near a nervous schnauzer.

🎲 Walk-In Roulette

Three people show up at 9AM without appointments. At 2PM, you're sitting there filing your nails because nobody came in. You want to help everyone, but walk-ins mess up your whole schedule. Meanwhile, your booked clients get cranky when they have to wait because someone "just stopped by real quick."

👻 The Great Disappearing Act

You block out two hours for Bella the bernedoodle. She's a no-show. You turned away walk-ins, and now you're watching tumbleweeds roll through your empty shop. Regular clients vanish for months, then show up expecting you to squeeze them in on Saturday afternoon. Meanwhile, you're trying to build a business around people who treat you like their backup plan.

💸 "I Thought That Was Free!"

"The nail trim is extra?" "You can't just squeeze in a quick bath?" "Why does a de-shed cost more than a regular cut?" You spend more time explaining your prices than actually grooming pets. Every service becomes a negotiation because people have no idea what they're actually booking.

⭐ Review Desert (Except for That One Crazy Lady)

Everyone says you're "amazing," but you have six Google reviews. One is from a woman convinced you turned her white poodle purple. Happy clients forget to leave reviews. Crazy clients never forget to complain. Meanwhile, the place down the street has 50 five-star reviews because they actually ask for them.

📱 "Text Me When Fluffy's Ready"

Of course you can text—except you're covered in fur, running late, and holding a blow dryer. Manual updates eat up your whole day. Every client wants play-by-play updates. You're already behind schedule, and now you're spending ten minutes texting progress reports instead of actually grooming.

✨

Here's What Actually Works for Pet Groomers

📅 Online Booking That Makes Sense

Clients see your services, pick their pet's needs, book their own appointments. No more phone tag while you're trying to work.

Book appointments without stopping mid-groom

⏰ Automated Reminders That Work

Text reminders the day before so people actually show up with the right pet at the right time.

Fewer no-shows, fewer "was that today?" calls

📋 Waitlist Magic for Cancellations

Someone cancels? Your waitlist automatically texts people who want that slot. Empty appointments get filled without you lifting a finger.

Turn cancellations into money

💰 Service Menus That Stop the Confusion

Clear prices, photos, "here's what's included (and what costs extra)." No more twenty-minute explanations for a basic bath.

Fewer arguments, happier pet parents

⭐ Automatic Review Requests

After every great groom, clients get a simple text asking for a review. More five-stars mean more people find you first.

Finally get credit for all those perfect cuts

📱 "Your Pet's Ready" Notifications

Automatic texts when pets are done. Clients know when to pick up, you don't waste time playing phone tag.

Less stress for everyone

📸 Simple Social Media Updates

Easy before/after photo sharing, monthly specials, and "time for your next groom" reminders that actually work.

Stay busy during slow seasons
